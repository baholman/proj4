<p><b>Gay pornography</b> is the representation of <a href="/wiki/Human_sexual_activity" title="Human sexual activity">sexual activity</a> between males. Its primary goal is <a href="/wiki/Sexual_arousal" title="Sexual arousal">sexual arousal</a> in its audience. <a href="/wiki/Softcore_pornography" title="Softcore pornography">Softcore</a> <a href="/wiki/Gay" title="Gay">gay</a> <a href="/wiki/Pornography" title="Pornography">pornography</a> also exists; it at one time constituted the <a href="/wiki/Pornography" title="Pornography">genre</a>, and may be produced as <a href="/wiki/Beefcake" title="Beefcake">beefcake</a> pornography for <a href="/wiki/Heterosexuality" title="Heterosexuality">heterosexual</a> female and <a href="/wiki/Homosexuality" title="Homosexuality">homosexual</a> male consumption.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although pornography has usually focused on heterosexuality due to the prevalence of the heterosexual orientation, <a href="/wiki/Homoeroticism" title="Homoeroticism">homoerotic</a> art and artifacts have a long history as well, reaching back to <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Greek antiquity</a>. Every <a href="/wiki/Recording_medium" class="mw-redirect" title="Recording medium">medium</a> has been used to represent <a href="/wiki/Men_who_have_sex_with_men" title="Men who have sex with men">sexual acts between men</a>. However, gay pornography in contemporary <a href="/wiki/Mass_media" title="Mass media">mass media</a> is mostly concentrated in the making of <a href="/wiki/Home_video" title="Home video">home videos</a> (including <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVDs</a>), cable broadcast and emerging <a href="/wiki/Video_on_demand" title="Video on demand">video on demand</a> and wireless markets, as well as images and movies for viewing on the Internet. (For printed gay erotica, see <a href="/wiki/Gay_pulp_fiction" title="Gay pulp fiction">gay pulp fiction</a>).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:312px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Swimming_hole.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Swimming_hole.jpg/310px-Swimming_hole.jpg" decoding="async" width="310" height="235" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Swimming_hole.jpg/465px-Swimming_hole.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Swimming_hole.jpg/620px-Swimming_hole.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2500" data-file-height="1897" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Swimming_hole.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i><a href="/wiki/The_Swimming_Hole" title="The Swimming Hole">The Swimming Hole</a></i> (1884–85) by the American artist <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Eakins" title="Thomas Eakins">Thomas Eakins</a> (1844–1916) is regarded as a masterpiece of American painting,<sup id="cite_ref-Swimming_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Swimming-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and has been called "the most finely designed of all his outdoor pictures".<sup id="cite_ref-G239_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-G239-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The painting has been "widely cited as a prime example of <a href="/wiki/Homoeroticism" title="Homoeroticism">homoeroticism</a> in American art".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Eakins himself appears in the water at bottom right – "in signature position, so to speak."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> According to Jonathan Weinberg, <i>The Swimming Hole</i> marked the beginning of homoerotic imagery in American art.<sup id="cite_ref-ad308_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ad308-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_modern_in_the_United_States">Early modern in the United States</span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Homoeroticism" title="Homoeroticism">Homoeroticism</a> has been present in photography and film since their invention. During much of that time, any sexual depiction had to remain <a href="/wiki/Subculture" title="Subculture">underground</a> because of <a href="/wiki/Obscenity" title="Obscenity">obscenity</a> laws. In particular, gay material might constitute evidence of an illegal act under <a href="/wiki/Sodomy_law" title="Sodomy law">sodomy laws</a> in many jurisdictions. This is no longer the case in the United States, since such laws were ruled unconstitutional by the Supreme Court in 2003 in <i><a href="/wiki/Lawrence_v._Texas" title="Lawrence v. Texas">Lawrence v. Texas</a></i>.
</p><p>However, hardcore pornographic motion pictures (<i>stag films,</i> as they were called prior to their legalization in 1970) were produced relatively early in the history of film. The first known pornographic film appears to have been made in Europe in 1908. The earliest known film to depict hardcore gay (and <a href="/wiki/Bisexuality" title="Bisexuality">bisexual</a>) sexual activity was the <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_France" title="Cinema of France">French film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Le_m%C3%A9nage_moderne_du_Madame_Butterfly" class="mw-redirect" title="Le ménage moderne du Madame Butterfly">Le ménage moderne du Madame Butterfly</a></i>, produced and released in 1920.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Most historians consider the first American stag film to be <i><a href="/wiki/A_Free_Ride" title="A Free Ride">A Free Ride</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> produced and released in 1915.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In the United States, however, hardcore gay sexual activity did not make it onto film until 1929's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Surprise_of_a_Knight" title="The Surprise of a Knight">The Surprise of a Knight</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Burger_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Burger-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Waugh_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Waugh-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Slade_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Slade-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Legal restrictions meant that early hardcore gay pornography was underground and that commercially available gay pornography primarily consisted of pictures of individual men either fully naked or wearing a <a href="/wiki/G-string" title="G-string">G-string</a>. Pornography in the 1940s and 1950s focused on athletic men or <a href="/wiki/Bodybuilding" title="Bodybuilding">bodybuilders</a> in statuesque poses. They were generally young, muscular, and with little or no visible body hair. These pictures were sold in physique magazines, also known as <a href="/wiki/Beefcake_magazine" title="Beefcake magazine">beefcake magazines</a>, allowing the reader to pass as a fitness enthusiast.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Model_Guild" title="Athletic Model Guild">Athletic Model Guild</a> (AMG), founded by photographer <a href="/wiki/Bob_Mizer" title="Bob Mizer">Bob Mizer</a> in 1945 in Los Angeles, was arguably the first studio to commercially produce material specifically for gay men and published the first magazine known as <i>Physique Pictorial</i> in 1951. <a href="/wiki/Tom_of_Finland" title="Tom of Finland">Tom of Finland</a> drawings are featured in many issues.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Mizer produced about a million images, and thousands of films and videos before he died on May 12, 1992. During the late 1960s and early 1970s, the advent of <a href="/wiki/16_mm_film" title="16 mm film">16 mm film</a> cameras enabled these photographers to produce underground movies of gay sex, male <a href="/wiki/Masturbation" title="Masturbation">masturbation</a>, or both. Sales of these products were either by mail-order or through more discreet channels. Some of the early gay pornographers would travel around the country selling their photographs and films out of their hotel rooms, with advertising only through word of mouth and magazine ads.
</p><p>The 1960s were also a period where many underground art-film makers integrated suggestive or overtly gay content in their work. <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Anger" title="Kenneth Anger">Kenneth Anger</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Scorpio_Rising_(film)" title="Scorpio Rising (film)">Scorpio Rising</a></i> (1963), <a href="/wiki/Andy_Warhol" title="Andy Warhol">Andy Warhol</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Blow_Job_(film)" title="Blow Job (film)">Blow Job</a></i> (1964) and <i>My Hustler</i> (1965), or <a href="/wiki/Paul_Morrissey" title="Paul Morrissey">Paul Morrissey</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Flesh_(1968_film)" title="Flesh (1968 film)">Flesh</a></i> (1968) are examples of <a href="/wiki/Experimental_film" title="Experimental film">experimental films</a> that are known to have influenced further gay pornographic films with their formal qualities and narratives. Tyler Gajewski is a noted actor and model of the period who appeared in Warhol's and Morrissey's films, as well as in Mizer's work at the AMG. Also of note is <a href="/wiki/Joe_Dallesandro" title="Joe Dallesandro">Joe Dallesandro</a>, who acted in hardcore gay pornographic films in his early 20s,<sup id="cite_ref-Burger_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Burger-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> posed nude for <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Scavullo" title="Francesco Scavullo">Francesco Scavullo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Bellas" title="Bruce Bellas">Bruce of L.A.</a> and Bob Mizer, and later acted for Warhol in films such as <i>Flesh</i>. Dallesandro was well-known to the public. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sexual_revolution">Sexual revolution</span></h3>
<p>During the 1960s, a series of <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_States" title="Supreme Court of the United States">United States Supreme Court</a> rulings created a more liberalized legal environment that allowed the commercialization of pornography. <i><a href="/wiki/MANual_Enterprises,_Inc._v._Day" class="mw-redirect" title="MANual Enterprises, Inc. v. Day">MANual Enterprises, Inc. v. Day</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> was the first decision by the United States Supreme Court which held that magazines consisting largely of photographs of nude or near-nude male models are not obscene within the meaning of § 1461. It was the first case in which the Court engaged in plenary review of a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Post_Office_Department" title="United States Post Office Department">Post Office Department</a> order holding obscene matter "nonmailable."<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The case is notable for its ruling that photographs of nude men are not obscene, an implication which opened up the U.S. Postal Service to nude male <a href="/wiki/Pornographic_magazine" title="Pornographic magazine">pornographic magazines</a>, especially those catering to gay men.<sup id="cite_ref-Waugh_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Waugh-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>

In fact, it was the first pornographic feature film of any sort. <i>Boys in the Sand</i> opened in a theater in New York City in December 1971 and played to a packed house with record-breaking box office receipts, preceding <i><a href="/wiki/Deep_Throat_(film)" title="Deep Throat (film)">Deep Throat</a></i>, the first commercial straight pornography film in America, which opened in June 1972. </p><p>The production of gay pornography films expanded during the 1970s. A few studios released films for the growing number of gay <a href="/wiki/Adult_movie_theater" title="Adult movie theater">adult movie theatres</a>, where men could also have sexual encounters. Often, the films reflected the sexual liberation that gay men were experiencing at the time, depicting the numerous public spaces where men engaged in sex: <a href="/wiki/Gay_bathhouse" title="Gay bathhouse">bathhouses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sex_club" title="Sex club">sex clubs</a>, beaches, etc.

<h3><span id="1970.E2.80.931985"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1970–1985">1970–1985</span></h3>
<p>From 1970–1985, commercial gay pornography was just getting set up to become the large industry that it is today.<sup id="cite_ref-Escoffier_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Escoffier-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Because it was in the fledgling stage, it recruited actors from the only network it had access to: the gay community.<sup id="cite_ref-Escoffier_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Escoffier-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Even among members of the gay community, people willing to act in gay porn were hard to come by due to the social stigma and implicated social risk of being publicly out.<sup id="cite_ref-Escoffier_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Escoffier-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s">1980s</span></h3>
<p>The 1980s were a period of transition for gay pornography film. The proliferation of <a href="/wiki/Videocassette_recorder" title="Videocassette recorder">VCRs</a> made pornography videos easily accessible, and, as their prices fell, the market for home videos aimed at adult viewers became more and more lucrative. By the mid-1980s, the standard was to release pornography movies directly on video, which meant the wide disappearance of pornography theaters. Furthermore, video recording being more affordable, a multitude of producers entered the market, making low-budget pornography videos.
</p><p>This shift from watching pornography as a public activity to doing so in private was also influenced by the discovery of HIV and the subsequent AIDS crisis. Public spaces for sex, such as theatres, became less attended when in the early 1980s it became a much riskier behavior. Masturbatory activities in the privacy of the home became a <a href="/wiki/Safe_sex" title="Safe sex">safe sex</a> practice in the midst of this health crisis.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Gay_movies" class="mw-redirect" title="Gay movies">Gay movies</a> of the 1970s had contained some exploration of novel ways to represent the sexual act. In the 1980s, by contrast, all movies seemed to be made under an unwritten set of rules and conventions. Most scenes would start with a few lines of dialogue, have performers engage in <a href="/wiki/Foreplay" title="Foreplay">foreplay</a> (<a href="/wiki/Fellatio" title="Fellatio">fellatio</a>), followed by <a href="/wiki/Anal_sex" title="Anal sex">anal penetration</a>, and ending with a visual climax <a href="/wiki/Close-up" title="Close-up">close-up</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ejaculation" title="Ejaculation">ejaculating</a> <a href="/wiki/Human_penis" title="Human penis">penises</a>, called a <i>money shot</i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Cum_shot" title="Cum shot">cum shot</a>.</i> Video technology allowed the recording of longer scenes than did the costly <a href="/wiki/Film_stock" title="Film stock">film stock</a>. Scenes were often composed of extended <a href="/wiki/Footage" title="Footage">footage</a> of the same act filmed from different <a href="/wiki/Shot_(filmmaking)" title="Shot (filmmaking)">shots</a> using multiple cameras. The quality of the picture and sound were often very poor.
</p><p>Major directors such as Matt Sterling, Eric Peterson, John Travis, and <a href="/wiki/William_Higgins_(director)" title="William Higgins (director)">William Higgins</a> set the standard for the models of the decade. The performers they cast were especially young, usually appearing to be around the ages of 22 or 23. Their bodies were slender and hairless, of the "swimmer's build" type, which contrasted with the older, bigger, and hairier man of the 1970s' gay pornography. Performer roles also evolved into the tight divisions of <a href="/wiki/Top,_bottom_and_versatile" title="Top, bottom and versatile"><i>tops</i> and <i>bottoms</i></a>. The <i>top</i> in <a href="/wiki/Anal_sex" title="Anal sex">anal sex</a> is the penetrating partner, who, in these films, typically has a more muscular body and the larger penis. The <i>bottom,</i> or receiver of anal sex, in the films is often smaller and sometimes more <a href="/wiki/Effeminacy" title="Effeminacy">effeminate</a>. The stars of the decade were almost always tops, while the bottoms were interchangeable (with the exception of <a href="/wiki/Joey_Stefano" title="Joey Stefano">Joey Stefano</a>, a popular star, who was more of a <i>bottom.</i>)
</p><p>This strict division between <i>tops</i> and <i>bottoms</i> may have reflected a preference by some of the popular directors of the decade to hire heterosexual men for their movies. Heterosexual men who perform gay sex for monetary reasons (commonly labeled <i><a href="/wiki/Gay-for-pay" title="Gay-for-pay">gay-for-pay</a></i>) were considered a rare commodity in the gay sex trade, but the biggest producers of the decade could afford them. Many critics attributed the conventionalization of gay pornography of the 1980s to this trend.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_1985">After 1985</span></h3>
<p>1985 was a pivotal year for gay porn because by then, the market had grown enough to make it a desirable field of work for not only gay men but also straight men. According to one estimate by porn director Chi Chi Larue, 60% of the actors in gay porn are actually straight. This incidence of straight men in gay porn is known as gay-for-pay and the ethics behind it and implications of it are highly disputed.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gay-for-pay">Gay-for-pay</span></h3>
<p>The authenticity and ethics behind gay-for-pay porn are highly disputed, even within the gay community. Viewers of gay porn in a survey by Escoffier reported a preference for authentic porn, which they define as exhibiting both erections and orgasms.<sup id="cite_ref-Escoffier_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Escoffier-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Escoffier argues if straight-identifying actors are able to deliver erections and orgasms to the set, their performance is classified as situational homosexuality; therefore, the porn itself is authentic gay porn.<sup id="cite_ref-Escoffier_20-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Escoffier-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Simon and Gagnon examine authenticity through scripts, arguing that porn actors follow learned behavioral sex scripts, so no porn is any more or less authentic than any other porn.<sup id="cite_ref-Escoffier_20-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Escoffier-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bareback">Bareback</span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Bareback_(sex)" title="Bareback (sex)">Bareback (sex)</a></div>
<p>Bareback gay pornography was standard in "pre-condom" films from the 1970s and early 1980s.  As awareness of the risk of AIDS developed, pornography producers came under pressure to use condoms, both for the health of the performers and to serve as role models for their viewers.  By the early 1990s new pornographic videos usually featured the use of condoms for anal sex.  However, beginning in the 1990s, an increasing number of studios have been devoted to the production of new films featuring men engaging in unprotected sex.<sup id="cite_ref-hivscandalbbc_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hivscandalbbc-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> For example, <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>-based studio <a href="/wiki/Treasure_Island_Media" title="Treasure Island Media">Treasure Island Media</a>, whose work focuses in this area, has produced bareback films since 1999. Other companies that do so include SEVP and <a href="/wiki/Eurocreme" title="Eurocreme">Eurocreme</a>. Mainstream gay pornographic studios such as <a href="/wiki/Kristen_Bjorn" title="Kristen Bjorn">Kristen Bjorn</a> Productions have featured the occasional bareback scene, such as in "El Rancho" between performers who are real-life partners. Other studios such as <a href="/wiki/Falcon_Entertainment" title="Falcon Entertainment">Falcon Entertainment</a> have also reissued older pre-condom films.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Also, mainstream studios that consistently use condoms for anal sex scenes may sometimes choose editing techniques that make the presence of condoms somewhat ambiguous and less visually evident, and thus may encourage viewers to fantasize that barebacking is taking place, even though the performers are following safer-sex protocols. (In contrast, some mainstream directors are conscientious about using close-up shots of condom packets being opened, etc., to help clearly establish for the viewer that the sex is not bareback.)
</p><p>Some scholars argue that while "barebacking" and "UAI" technically both mean the same thing, they have different undertones.<sup id="cite_ref-Mowlabocus_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mowlabocus-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> With the increased use of the term "barebacking", the term has been adopted for marketing purposes.<sup id="cite_ref-Mowlabocus_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mowlabocus-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> This is because "Unprotected Anal Intercourse" makes a direct connection between unprotected sex and the risk of contracting diseases like HIV/AIDS.<sup id="cite_ref-Mowlabocus_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mowlabocus-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> However, although "bareback" seems to have become the favorable term, studies show that both terms ultimately have equally negative implications.<sup id="cite_ref-Mowlabocus_38-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mowlabocus-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> In a study where participants were shown two different scenes featuring anal sex, the significance of the words "bareback" and "UAI" became apparent.<sup id="cite_ref-Mowlabocus_38-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mowlabocus-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The first scene featured group sex in which several men were on top engaging in intercourse with one man on the bottom.<sup id="cite_ref-Mowlabocus_38-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mowlabocus-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The men on top were in their mid-30s and of varying ethnicities while the man on the bottom was around 18 years old.<sup id="cite_ref-Mowlabocus_38-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mowlabocus-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The second scene featured two men both in their 20s in a living room setting.<sup id="cite_ref-Mowlabocus_38-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mowlabocus-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> During the interview, the participants were much more reluctant to classify the second scene as "bareback" or "UAI", than they were for the first scene.<sup id="cite_ref-Mowlabocus_38-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mowlabocus-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Participants readily used "bareback" to describe the first scene in which there were clear contrasts in race, age, and power.<sup id="cite_ref-Mowlabocus_38-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mowlabocus-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The participants described the second scene as being more "meaningful and romantic" and hence more likely to use a condom to protect the other.<sup id="cite_ref-Mowlabocus_38-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mowlabocus-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The implication of this study is that the term "bareback" ultimately does have a dark meaning as it relates to HIV/AIDS, regardless if it does not mention protection in its name.<sup id="cite_ref-Mowlabocus_38-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mowlabocus-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Thus, studies have shown that barebacking is decreasing in popularity within the gay subculture.<sup id="cite_ref-Mowlabocus_38-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mowlabocus-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Bareback pornography does not necessarily encourage more unprotected anal sex in reality, nor do all men who participate in anal sex necessarily want to have unprotected sex.<sup id="cite_ref-Mowlabocus_38-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mowlabocus-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> What is clear is that there is still a sense of risk among participants of anal sex.<sup id="cite_ref-Mowlabocus_38-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mowlabocus-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
